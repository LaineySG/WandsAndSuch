Step 1: Name, Level, and Year
Character Name:

Your character's level determines their strengths, skills and starting spells. Typically, a character's level will be equal to their year at school. An adult's level will typically be 7 or higher depending on their age and level of knowledge. Professors may be level 9 or 10, for example. If you are playing starting as a first year student, your level should be 1 to reflect that they are in first-year.

You character's year will typically start at 1. A graduate or teacher will not have a school year.

Select character virtues

A character in Wands & Such has virtues, which determine their skills and abilities in different scenarios. These Virtues include smarts, grit, charm, vigor and loyalty. A "learning bonus" affects how quickly your character picks up new spells and how easily they can become a practiced caster of that spell.

Smarts

Your character's smarts determines their knowledge, how quickly their learn, and their "book-smarts". A character high in smarts may be found in a library studying, working on a new charm or magical creation, perfecting an incantation or divining the future to be prepared for what lies ahead.


  • Magical Theory
  • Muggle Knowledge
  • Insightfulness
  • Learning bonus to charmwork
  • Learning bonus to divinatory magic (arithmancy, astronomoy, divination)

Grit

Your character's grit determines their ability to take hits, solve problems and face adversity, and help others do the same. A character with high grit could be found helping friends, solving problems, or immersed in creation of a complicated potion. A character with high grit will rarely be caught off-guard.


  • Health and defense
  • Determination in the face of adversity
  • Investigative skills
  • Learning bonus to defensive magic
  • Learning bonus to potionmaking

Charm

Your character's charm determines their charisma, how easily they can get along with others and weave the social fabric to match their desires. A charming character could typically be found with a group of friends, the type of person you would definitely prefer as a friend than as an enemy.


  • Persuasiveness
  • Deception
  • Intimidation
  • Learning bonus to hexes, curses, and jinxes (an offensive subset of spells)

Vigor

Your character's vigor determines their athleticism, fitness, and offensive magic. A character high in vigor could often be found on the quidditch pitch, or in a duelling club.


  • Athletics
  • Dexterity
  • Stealth
  • Learning bonus to offensive magic
  • Learning bonus to transfiguration magic

Loyalty

Your character's loyalty determines their ability to befriend creatures, perceive things other may not, and have luck on their side. A loyal character could be found healing and helping others, forming strong friendships, or taking care of advanced magical creatures and magical plants. A loyal character may seem to attract positivity like a magnet.


  • Magical Theory
  • Muggle Knowledge
  • Insightfulness
  • Learning Bonus to charmwork
  • Learning bonus to divinatory magic (arithmancy, astronomoy, divination)


When you have some idea of what sort of character you want to play, choose your stats below. As a level 1 character, you have 3 options:


1) Distribute either 24 points among the stats

2) Distribute 22 points and choose a perk for your character later (Your perk will be chosen in the next step).

3) If you want to roll stats, roll 8D6 and choose the highest 5 die, and distribute the sum of those 5 die in any way you choose. If you choose not to take a perk, add an additional 2 to the sum. The digital stat roller will also assign the numbers - feel free to reassign however you wish.


If you are creating a character above level 1, you gain 2 virtue points per level, or 1 perk per level.

Each virtue has a virtue modifier. This will be automatically calculated based on your virtue score. A virtue score modifier can also be manually calculated with the formula (ceil(virtue score / 2) - 2). You can also find a table in the rulebook.
