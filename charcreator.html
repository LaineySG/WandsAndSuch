Step 1: Name, Level, and Year
Character Name:

Your character's level determines their strengths, skills and starting spells. Typically, a character's level will be equal to their year at school. An adult's level will typically be 7 or higher depending on their age and level of knowledge. Professors may be level 9 or 10, for example. If you are playing starting as a first year student, your level should be 1 to reflect that they are in first-year.

Step 2: Select character virtues

A character in Wands & Such has virtues, which determine their skills and abilities in different scenarios. These Virtues include smarts, grit, charm, vigor and loyalty. A "learning bonus" affects how quickly your character picks up new spells and how easily they can become a practiced caster of that spell.

Smarts

Your character's smarts determines their knowledge, how quickly they learn, and their "book-smarts". A character high in smarts may be found in a library studying, working on a new charm or magical creation, perfecting an incantation or divining the future to be prepared for what lies ahead.


  • Magical Theory
  • Muggle Knowledge
  • Insightfulness
  • Learning bonus to charmwork
  • Learning bonus to divinatory magic (arithmancy, astronomoy, divination)

Grit

Your character's grit determines their ability to take hits, solve problems and face adversity, and help others do the same. A character with high grit could be found helping friends, solving problems, or immersed in creation of a complicated potion. A character with high grit will rarely be caught off-guard.


  • Health and defense
  • Determination in the face of adversity
  • Investigative skills
  • Learning bonus to defensive magic
  • Learning bonus to potionmaking

Charm

Your character's charm determines their charisma, how easily they can get along with others and weave the social fabric to match their desires. A charming character could typically be found with a group of friends, the type of person you would definitely prefer as a friend than as an enemy.


  • Persuasiveness
  • Deception
  • Intimidation
  • Learning bonus to hexes, curses, and jinxes (an offensive subset of spells)

Vigor

Your character's vigor determines their athleticism, fitness, and offensive magic. A character high in vigor could often be found on the quidditch pitch, or in a duelling club.


  • Athletics
  • Dexterity
  • Stealth
  • Learning bonus to offensive magic
  • Learning bonus to transfiguration magic

Loyalty

Your character's loyalty determines their ability to befriend creatures, perceive things others may not, and have luck on their side. A loyal character could be found healing and helping others, forming strong friendships, or taking care of advanced magical creatures and magical plants. A loyal character may seem to attract positivity like a magnet.


  • Magical Theory
  • Muggle Knowledge
  • Insightfulness
  • Learning bonus to charmwork
  • Learning bonus to divinatory magic (arithmancy, astronomoy, divination)

1) Distribute 24 points among the stats

2) Distribute 22 points and choose a perk for your character later (Your perk will be chosen in the next step).

3) If you want to roll stats, roll 8D6 and choose the highest 5 die, and distribute the sum of those 5 die in any way you choose. If you choose not to take a perk, add an additional 2 to the sum. The digital stat roller will also assign the numbers - feel free to reassign however you wish.


If you are creating a character above level 1, you gain 2 virtue points per level, or 1 perk per level.

Each virtue has a virtue modifier. This will be automatically calculated based on your virtue score. A virtue score modifier can also be manually calculated with the formula (ceil(virtue score / 2) - 2). You can also find a table in the rulebook.

Step 6: Heritage

Your character's heritage defines their upbringing, starting knowledge, and home-life. A character can be born to a muggle (non-wizarding) family, a mixed family with 1 wizard or witch, and 1 muggle parent, or a pureblood family with 2 wizard or witch parents.

Muggle-Born Heritage

A Muggle-born character is typically entirely unaware of the wizarding world until receiving their acceptance letter to Hogwarts. Muggle-borns have in-depth knowledge of muggles and muggle society and are typically determined to prove themselves.


  • Gain a +5 heritage bonus to your Muggle Knowledge skill for being raised by muggles.
  • Gain a +3 heritage bonus to your Luck skill
  • Gain a +1 heritage bonus to your Determination skill (To reflect adversity).

Mixed Heritage

A character with both wizarding and muggle parents is typically knowledgeable about both societies, and has some level of magical expertise.


  • Gain a +2 heritage bonus to your Muggle Knowledge skill
  • Gain a +1 heritage bonus to your Magical Theory skill.
  • Add 1 casting level to a single category of magic (as seen under “casting levels” on your character sheet).
  • Choose any 2 spells (Charm, Hex, Curse, Jinx) or potions from the first-year list and add them to your spell-book as “Practiced”.

Pure-blood Heritage

A pure-blood character is typically very knowledgeable about magic and quite skilled in it, having picked up a few tricks while growing up and watching their family.


  • Gain a +2 heritage bonus to “Magical Theory”.
  • Add 1 casting level each to any two categories of magic.
  • Choose 3 spells (Charm, Hex, Curse, Jinx) or potions from the first-year list, and add them to your spell-book as “Practiced”.

Step 8: Signature Spell

Each student may choose one “signature spell” which can only be changed once per term, and can be chosen at any time. This signature spell gains a +1 modifier on top of its “practice-level modifier”. You can note this under your “spell book”, though it’s not recommended to choose it immediately.


Step 9: Starting Galleons

You begin with 100 + 6D8 galleons to buy supplies. Pocket money will not arrive until the second week of the term. If your character is starting with the "Wealthy" or "Heir/Heiress" perk, they can use their favour immediately.
